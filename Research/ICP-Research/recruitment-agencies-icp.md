# Define Recruitment Agencies / Headhunters

## 1. What Recruitment Agencies Do?

Recruitment agencies help companies find, screen, interview, and hire candidates. They usually work as intermediaries between the company and the job seeker.

They handle:

- searching for candidates
- reviewing CVs
- contacting candidates
- screening candidates
- scheduling interviews
- following up with candidates and clients
- managing candidate pipelines
- helping companies close hiring faster

Recruitment CRM/ATS tools are used to organize candidates, track hiring stages, and manage relationships with both candidates and clients. Recruitment pipelines commonly include stages such as Applied/New Applicant, Screening, Interview, Offer, Hired, and Rejected.

---

## 2. Recruitment Agency Workflow

Typical workflow:

### 1. Client sends hiring request:
The company asks the agency to find candidates for a specific role.

### 2. Agency defines job requirements:
Recruiters understand skills, experience, salary, location, and urgency.

### 3. Candidate sourcing:
Recruiters search on LinkedIn, job boards, internal database, referrals, or cold outreach.

### 4. Candidate screening:
Recruiter reviews CVs, checks skills, asks basic questions, and filters weak candidates.

### 5. Interview scheduling:
Recruiter coordinates time between candidate and client.

### 6. Interview follow-up:
Recruiter follows up with candidates and clients after interviews.

### 7. Offer stage:
If the client likes the candidate, the agency helps with offer communication.

### 8. Hired or rejected:
Candidate is either hired, rejected, or saved for future opportunities.

End-to-end recruitment usually covers the full hiring process from identifying the hiring need to selection, offer, and onboarding.

---

## 3. Main Pain Points

# Pain Point 1: Candidate Screening

### Problem:
Recruiters receive many CVs, but not all candidates are qualified. Reviewing each CV manually takes a lot of time.

### Why it matters:
Bad screening wastes recruiter time and may cause strong candidates to be missed.

### Automation solution:
Use AI to scan CVs, match candidates with job requirements, and give each candidate a score.

### Feature ideas:

- AI CV parsing
- Candidate-job matching score
- automatic skill extraction
- knockout questions
- shortlist recommendation

### Workflow:
Candidate applies → system reads CV → compares skills with job → gives score → recruiter sees best candidates first.

AI workflows for staffing agencies can support job description generation, chatbot pre-screening, candidate scoring, and interview support.

---

# Pain Point 2: Interview Scheduling

### Problem:
Recruiters waste time sending many emails to find a suitable interview time.

### Automation solution:
Create automated scheduling where candidates choose available time slots.

### Feature ideas:

- calendar integration
- auto-send interview links
- reminder emails/SMS
- rescheduling button
- automatic status update to “Interview Scheduled”

### Workflow:
Candidate passes screening → system sends booking link → candidate selects time → calendar updates → reminders sent automatically.

Scheduling automation helps reduce repetitive email rounds and saves recruiter time.

---

# Pain Point 3: Follow-up Emails

### Problem:
Recruiters may forget to follow up with candidates or clients, especially when managing many roles.

### Automation solution:
Use automated email sequences based on candidate stage.

### Feature ideas:

- auto follow-up after interview
- rejection email templates
- client feedback reminder
- candidate status update email
- personalized email templates

### Workflow:
Interview completed → no feedback after 2 days → system sends reminder to client → candidate receives update automatically.

---

# Pain Point 4: Managing Candidate Pipelines

### Problem:
Recruiters handle many candidates at different stages, which can become messy in spreadsheets or old CRM systems.

### Automation solution:
Use a visual CRM pipeline with automatic stage updates and task reminders.

### Feature ideas:

- drag-and-drop candidate pipeline
- pipeline dashboard
- overdue task alerts
- candidate history
- stage-based automation
- recruiter performance reports

### Workflow:
Candidate added → moves through stages → system tracks progress → recruiter sees bottlenecks and urgent actions.

A hiring pipeline helps recruiters visually track candidates through hiring stages and identify bottlenecks.

## 5. CRM Pipeline Design

Recommended pipeline stages:

1. **New Applicant:** Candidate is added from LinkedIn, job board, referral, or manual entry.

2. **Screening:** CV and basic qualifications are reviewed.

3. **Shortlisted:** Candidate is suitable and ready to be sent to the client.

4. **Interview Scheduled:** Interview time is confirmed.

5. **Interview Completed:** Candidate finished interview and is waiting for feedback.

6. **Offer Sent:** Client wants to hire and offer is sent.

7. **Hired:** Candidate accepted the offer.

8. **Rejected:** Candidate was not selected.

9. **Talent Pool:** Candidate is not suitable now but may fit future jobs.
